Introducing Operating System

Thread in operating system

What is thread?

A thread in an operating system is the smallest unit of execution within a process. It's a sequential flow of task. Threads are also termed as lightweight process as they share common resources.

Example: While playing movie on a device the audio and video are controlled by different threads in background.

Why do we need thread?

  1. Since thread use the same data and code, the operational cost is low in thread.
  2. Creating and terminating thread is faster compared to creating or terminating a process.
  3. Context switching is faster compared to processes.
  4. Thread improve CPU utilization, reducing idle time.
  5. Threads communicate easily via shared memory.
